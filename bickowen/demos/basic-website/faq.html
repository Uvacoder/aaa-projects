Frequently asked questions
Why wireless electricity?
You see, wireless electricity isn't a new concept. However, wireless electricity hasn't been fully attempted on a consumer-level. In other words, the concept of wireless electricity has existed for a long time but has never been a staple of residential and business culture. There are many benefits to wireless electricity including:
    • You can place compatible items anywhere and have them still be powered.
    • No more wire clutter!
    • Less of fire hazard.
    • If rodents get into the house, they can't chew inexistant wires.
Why hasn't it been done before?
On a small developmental scale, it has. There are small companies that are actively trying to create the technology, but none have the same mass market ideology in mind. Not only that, but their systems are run through the house's already wired electricity. The idea of Neutron is to completely 110% get rid of wires.
How much will it cost to make?
Based on current rough estimates, it's expected to cost $50,000,000 USD until the powerstation is completely developed and ready to go mass market.
How is the powerstation powered?
The sun. The powerstation will be compatible with solar roof projects and regular solar panels. Compatibility with other forms of clean energy is currently being considered.
What will happen if the powerstation stops working? Will the client completely lose power?
No, unless somehow the energy reservation system is also damaged.
How much will it cost the customer?
We estimate it will cost a customer $5,000 USD per powerstation.
